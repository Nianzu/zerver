Personal Project - Scylla

In an attempt to better understand neural networks (and make up for my abject failure of a chess engine in 2023), I built Scylla. Scylla is a multi-agent (its got multiple networks) neral network based chess engine. Its design is based on this paper by Barak Oshri and Nishith Khandwala of Stanford University. The novel idea borrowed here is the used of 7 separate neural networks (one for piece selection, and one of each of the 6 pieces in chess). The composition of the piece selection matrix with the highest values from the relevant move selector matrices decides which piece is moved where on a given turn.

Read more about the project on github!

Methodology

Each of the networks in Scylla was trained on relevant moves from 20,000 games between players of 2000 elo or better. There are some issues with how I do validation, so I don't believe tracking validation is a good way to find a stopping point for training. For now, each network was trained to 200 epochs.
